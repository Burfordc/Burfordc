3D Printing and Additive Manufacturing

3D printing is a form of additive maufacturing. Additive manufacturing is a process in which layers of a material are placed on top of eachother.

This is different to processes such as subtractive manufacturing. A common example of subtractive is milling or the stock removal method.

Additive manufacturing allows for certain objects and designs to be created in a more efficent way.

It is also possible for this process to create objects that are impossible for the Subtractive manufacturing process to make.

The Makerbot printers used at school have a build plate restriction of 25.2 L x 19.9 W x 15.0 H centimeters or 9.9 L x 7.8 W x 5.9 H inches.

The first thing to be made was a combination of an object and my face.

My head was scanned and then overlayed onto the hood of a Nissan 350z.

This process was completed using a program called Meshmixer.

Meshmixer allows the user to combine, edit, and modify 3d designs to be 3D printed.
